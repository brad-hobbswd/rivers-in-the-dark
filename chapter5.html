CHAPTER 5

The Test of Faith

Weeks passed after Brad’s midnight vision, each day bringing small but undeniable changes. He noticed his reactions softening at work, moments of quiet reflection with Gabe, and a growing awareness of God’s presence guiding him. Yet the real test had yet to come.

One afternoon, Brad received an email from a local community center that worked with at-risk youth. They were seeking volunteers to mentor and share stories of personal growth. Brad’s first thought was hesitation. He doubted his ability to make a meaningful impact. Could he really reach people who had been abandoned, overlooked, or hardened by life?

He called Justin, seeking perspective. “I’m not sure I can do this,” Brad admitted. “These kids… they’ve been through so much. I’m just one person.”

Justin’s voice was calm but firm. “Brad, remember the bird you freed? Remember your vision? God isn’t asking you to do it alone. He’s equipping you. You just need to step forward.”

Brad looked at Gabe playing quietly nearby, assembling a small tower of blocks with careful precision. He felt a surge of determination. “You’re right,” he said. “I need to do this; for me, for Gabe, and for those kids.”

The next Saturday, Brad arrived at the community center with Gabe holding his hand. The center was bustling with activity; kids laughing, staff coordinating programs, the smell of fresh paint and snacks filling the air. Brad felt nervous but reminded himself of the words from his vision: Your voice is for the unreachable.

Gabe stayed close, whispering encouragement. “You can do it, Daddy. Just tell them your story.”

Brad took a deep breath and approached a small group of teenagers seated on folding chairs. Their expressions were wary, some arms crossed, eyes skeptical. Brad’s heart raced. He introduced himself simply. “Hi, my name is Brad, and I want to share a little about my journey, mistakes I’ve made, and how trusting God helped me find direction.”

He spoke slowly, honestly, about his struggles with purpose, fear, and failure. He described freeing the sparrow with Gabe, his dream of the dark forest, and the midnight vision of the stadium. The teens listened silently, their eyes widening as they heard someone being vulnerable without pretense or judgment.

At one point, Gabe tugged on Brad’s sleeve. “Daddy, tell them about the times you wanted to quit but didn’t,” he whispered.

Brad smiled at his son and nodded, continuing. “There were many times I wanted to give up. I felt trapped, like I would never be enough. But trusting God, listening to His Spirit, and taking small steps of obedience changed everything.”

By the end of the session, a quiet energy filled the room. A teen raised her hand hesitantly. “You… you really mean it? That we can do better?”

Brad nodded, voice steady. “Yes. You’re not defined by the mistakes of your past. God can help you see the way forward, even when it feels impossible.”

Later, as they left the center, Gabe walked beside Brad, his hand in his father’s. “Daddy, did you see them? They were listening!”

Brad smiled, feeling a deep sense of fulfillment. “Yes, buddy. They were. And that’s just the beginning.”

On the drive home, Brad reflected on the day. The Holy Ghost had guided him, giving him the courage to speak about life, the words to encourage hearts, and the calm to remain confident despite fear. He realized that faith was not just personal; it was active, relational, and transformative.

That night, he knelt beside Gabe’s bed. “Jesus, thank You for today. Thank You for showing me that even when I feel small, You can use me. Teach me to continue walking with courage, speaking life, and guiding Gabe in Your ways.”

Gabe hugged him tightly. “I think God is proud of you, Daddy. And I’m proud of you too.”

Brad felt a surge of gratitude. The Holy Ghost was not just attractive in presence but powerful in action. Through obedience, humility, and trust, God could reach the unreachable, starting with his own family and extending to the lives he touched that day.
