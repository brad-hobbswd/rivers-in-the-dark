CHAPTER 3

The Storm Within

Monday arrived gray and humid, the city streets slick from the lingering rain. Brad’s commute felt heavier than usual. His thoughts churned with the memory of the sparrow he had freed over the weekend and Gabe’s innocent words about freedom. Yet work pressures loomed large, threatening to smother that flicker of hope.

The morning passed uneventfully until Brad’s manager called him into the office. The tension in the air was thick, and Brad’s stomach tightened.

“Brad, I need to discuss the Johnson project,” his manager said, flipping through a stack of papers. “Your analysis was incomplete. We cannot move forward like this. You need to get it right next time.”

Brad swallowed. He could feel the familiar surge of anger and frustration, the old pattern of self-doubt creeping in. Normally, he would have snapped back or gone home feeling defeated. But today, something inside him shifted. He remembered the dream of the dark forest, the soft voice whispering, I am with you. Do not fear.

He nodded slowly. “I understand. I’ll review it again and make the necessary adjustments,” he said, keeping his voice calm. His manager looked slightly surprised at his composure but said nothing more.

During lunch, Brad stepped outside. Rain began to fall lightly, cold droplets splashing on his face. He closed his eyes and breathed deeply, whispering a silent prayer: “Jesus, guide me. Let Your Spirit shape my words and actions, even when I feel frustration or fear.”

The rain fell steadily, washing away the tension he had carried in his chest. When he returned to his desk, a coworker, noticing his calm demeanor, commented quietly, “You seem different lately. Even when things go wrong, you stay… steady. What’s changed?”

Brad smiled faintly, thinking of the sparrow, the vision he had not yet fully understood, and the quiet stirrings of the Holy Ghost in his heart. “I’m learning to trust, little by little,” he said softly.

That evening, Brad returned home, greeted by Gabe’s excited voice. “Daddy! Look! I made a fort with my blankets!”

Brad chuckled, stepping inside. “That looks amazing, buddy. Can I help you build the next level?”

As they worked together, Brad shared his day in simple terms. “Sometimes, Daddy gets frustrated at work. But I’m learning that God can help me stay calm and make better choices.”

Gabe nodded solemnly, his small face thoughtful. “I think He’s proud of you, Daddy.”

Brad felt a surge of warmth. The Holy Ghost was at work, shaping him not only in his own life but in his son’s perception of faith. He realized that spiritual transformation was slow and quiet, like a river cutting through stone, unseen but powerful.

After dinner, Brad tucked Gabe into bed, brushing his hair from the boy’s forehead. “You know, buddy, God works in ways we can’t always see. He teaches us through small things, like freeing a bird, or staying calm when we want to get angry.”

Gabe yawned, eyes heavy. “I think I’m learning too, Daddy. I want to be brave like you.”

Brad kissed his son’s forehead. “You already are, Gabe. You already are.”

Later, sitting in the living room, Brad journaled his thoughts: Faith is not always a loud experience. Sometimes it is a whisper, a choice to stay calm, a small act of trust. The Spirit is guiding me, preparing me for the path ahead.

He closed his journal and prayed again. “Jesus, let Your river flow in me. Let it guide my actions, shape my words, and touch others through me. Teach me to trust You in every part of life.”

The storm, outside continued, rain dripping steadily, but inside, Brad felt a deep, unshakable peace. He understood that the Holy Ghost was shaping him through daily life, through work stress, parenting, and small acts of obedience. Change was happening, even if he could not yet see the full picture.
