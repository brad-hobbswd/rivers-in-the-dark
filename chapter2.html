CHAPTER 2

The First Sign

Saturday morning arrived gray and damp, the rain from the night before leaving the sidewalks slick and glistening. Brad loaded Gabe into the car, the boy chattering endlessly about superheroes and dinosaurs, trying to convince his dad that puddle-jumping was a competitive sport.

“Daddy, I’m faster than a cheetah!” Gabe shouted as they stepped into the park, boots squelching in the mud.

Brad laughed despite the weight in his chest. He had been carrying weeks of stress from work, bills, and his own self-doubt. But watching Gabe run freely, unburdened by adult worries, reminded him that life was meant to be lived in moments, not just survived.

As they walked toward a clearing near the small soccer field, Gabe suddenly stopped, pointing excitedly. “Daddy, look! It’s stuck!”

Brad followed his son’s gaze to see a tiny sparrow tangled in a discarded piece of netting. Its wings flapped frantically, eyes wide with panic. Brad crouched carefully, whispering soothing words to calm the bird while he untangled the net. Gabe watched intently, holding his breath.

“There you go, little one. Fly,” Brad said as the bird stretched its wings and soared into the gray sky.

Gabe’s eyes sparkled. “Daddy! You saved it! Now it’s free! Just like… like Jesus helps people be free!”

Brad’s chest warmed. The simplicity of his son’s observation struck him deeply. Freedom wasn’t just about physical escape; it was spiritual, emotional, and relational. In that moment, he realized how often he had felt trapped by fear, doubt, and past failures. And yet, the act of freeing something small mirrored the larger freedom Jesus offered in their lives.

They continued their walk, rain dripping from the brim of Brad’s jacket. Gabe ran ahead to splash in puddles, laughing, while Brad walked more slowly, letting the sensation of the wet air and the bird’s flight settle in his heart.

At lunch, they stopped at a small café, Gabe devouring his pancakes while Brad sipped coffee, eyes distant. The warmth from the café, combined with the laughter of other families, reminded him that life contained small, beautiful moments even amid uncertainty.

Later, back home, Brad tucked Gabe into bed. “You know, buddy,” he said softly, brushing the hair from his son’s forehead, “sometimes we all feel trapped. But God can help us be free, in ways we don’t always see.”

Gabe yawned but whispered, “Like Jesus, Daddy?”

“Yes, like Jesus,” Brad said, holding him close for a moment longer than usual.

After Gabe drifted to sleep, Brad stayed in the living room, the city lights shimmering through the window. He picked up his journal and wrote slowly: Freedom starts small. One act, one choice, one prayer. God is moving, even when I cannot see it yet.

Then he prayed, voice low but certain. “Jesus, help me see the ways You want me to walk. Teach me to guide Gabe and myself into Your freedom.”

For the first time in weeks, he felt the stirrings of hope; like a quiet river beginning to flow, subtle but undeniable. The Holy Ghost was at work, attracting him, guiding him, and whispering that transformation begins with small, faithful steps.
